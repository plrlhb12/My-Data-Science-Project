HIV/AIDS epidemic in the United States

HIV: stands for Human Immunodeficiency Virus.

AIDS: stands for Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome. It is a advanced stage of HIV infection.

Global HIV Facts

HIV is a significant and preventable public health problem in the United States and world wide. As of 2017, approximately 36.9 million people were living with HIV, which is the 0.8% of population. Tens of millions of people have died of AIDS-related causes since the beginning of the epidemic. Due to the global efforts to address this challenge during the past past two decades, the number of people newly diagnoised with HIV, and the number of AIDS-related deaths have declined over the years.

US HIV Facts

In the United States, an estimated 1,122,900 adults and adolescents (about 0.4% of population) were living with HIV at the end of 2015. Of those, 162,500 (15%) had not received a diagnosis. In 2016, there were 15,807 deaths among people with diagnosed HIV in the United States. In 2017, 38,739 people are newly diagnosed with HIV.
